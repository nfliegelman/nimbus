## 2. What the program does

`kalshi_weather.py` is a single Python script that, on each run:
1. Pulls open Kalshi daily high/low temperature markets across 20 US cities (`Climate and Weather`, series `KXHIGHT*` and `KXLOWT*`).
2. Builds a fair-value probability for every temperature bucket from a pooled multi-model ENSEMBLE (Open-Meteo GFS + ECMWF + ICON + GEM, ~143 members). Members are first CALIBRATED: shifted by a per-city bias correction learned from Kalshi settlements, then each member is dressed with a Gaussian kernel whose width is learned from realized errors (Wang-Bishop second-moment matching). Bucket probability = average kernel mass inside the bucket's real interval [floor-0.5, cap+0.5), matching NWS half-up rounding. Daily highs/lows are taken over the NWS Climate Report day (Local STANDARD Time year-round, so 1:00 AM to 12:59 AM clock time during DST), because that is the exact window Kalshi settles on.
3. Flags edges (model prob minus Kalshi price, after spread and fee), guarded (section 4).
4. Sizes each play in UNITS (2u / 1.5u / 1u / no bet) from a confidence score plus a win-probability cap (section 5).
5. LOGS predictions, then RESOLVES past ones using Kalshi's own settled `result` and `expiration_value` (authoritative win/loss and margin of victory). Plays FREEZE at their first non-empty log (the tracker scores the first board that showed the play); buckets, mean, and sigma keep refreshing on later runs so calibration always learns from the freshest forecast.
6. Writes two dashboards into `docs/` for GitHub Pages: `index.html` (today's bets) and `results.html` (performance tracker with inline SVG charts, per-city and per-unit breakdowns, Brier vs market, margins, raw table).
7. Persists everything to `weather_state.json`.

Runs on **GitHub Actions** (cloud) three times daily (morning board, a 14:43 UTC capture run right after Kalshi lists next-day markets, evening board + settlement) and publishes to **GitHub Pages**. Also runs locally (opens a browser). One file, auto-detecting via the `CI` env var. Running in the cloud is WHY the owner's office firewall is a non-issue: fetches happen on GitHub's network, the owner only ever views github.io pages.

## 4. The guards (DELIBERATE, do not remove)

Every one of these prevents a fake edge that looked real during development. If you find yourself removing a guard to produce more plays, you are going the wrong way.

- **Timing guard (`realized`).** A same-day LOW is already realized (the low prints at dawn), and a same-day HIGH is realized after `INTRADAY_HIGH_CUTOFF` local hours (default 14). Realized markets are never scored as edges; you cannot forecast weather that already happened. Local hour is derived from Open-Meteo's `utc_offset_seconds` (no `zoneinfo`/`tzdata` dependency, which matters on Windows).
- **Bias guard (`biased`).** If the model's mean temp is offset from the market's implied mean by more than `BIAS_TOL` (2.0 deg F), the whole city is suppressed from plays. A center-of-mass disagreement is almost always my station coordinates not matching Kalshi's resolution station, not real alpha. Real edge lives in the SHAPE of the distribution once the means line up. The per-city bias panel on the Results tab is how the owner learns which cities need coordinate fixes.
- **Single-winner buckets.** Kalshi temp buckets are exhaustive and mutually exclusive, so model probabilities across a ladder sum to ~1. This program only handles temp ladders. Do NOT copy this logic onto multi-winner or nested markets (album releases, "before date X" ladders) where probabilities do not sum to 1; that manufactured huge fake edges in earlier prediction-market work.
- **Favorite-longshot direction.** Retail overpays for unlikely YES outcomes. Buying a YES longshot is the trap; taking the NO side of an overpriced longshot is the good, high-win-probability fade. The win-probability cap (section 5) encodes this so a big edge on a low-YES-probability bucket cannot get a big size.
- **Cost gate.** A bucket is only a play when net edge (model prob minus price, minus half-spread minus Kalshi fee minus 1c buffer) clears `PLAY_NET_EDGE`, open interest clears `MIN_OI`, lead is within `MAX_LEAD_DAYS`, and price is not at the 0/100 rails.
- **LST settlement window.** Daily highs/lows are computed over the NWS Climate Report day (Local Standard Time year-round). During DST that day is 1:00 AM to 12:59 AM local clock. This lives in `fetch_members` and is a settlement-correctness guard, not a style choice.
- **Local-clock lead (audit batch 1).** `lead` and the local hour used by the realized guard come from each CITY's clock (Open-Meteo `utc_offset_seconds`, fallback `STD_OFFSET_H`), never the runner's. GitHub runners are UTC: after 00:00 UTC the runner's date is tomorrow in every US zone, so runner-date leads mislabeled every market by a day on evening runs and the realized guard wrongly killed all next-day logging. Do not revert to `dt.date.today()` for leads.
- **Play freeze (audit batch 1).** Once a prediction record has a non-empty `plays` list, later runs must never rewrite it (`plays_lead`, `plays_logged_at`, `plays_model_version` stamp the decision moment). Without this, each run overwrote the logged plays with the freshest board and the tracker could diverge from what the owner actually bet. Buckets and mean deliberately still refresh. An empty plays list stays open so an edge that appears later can be picked up once, then locks.
- **Integrity gate (audit item 0.8, owner-approved, batch 7).** Before pricing: a pull under `GATE_MIN_LADDERS=25` aborts the run (exit 2); a ladder failing the structural contiguity check, a city/day under `GATE_MIN_MEMBERS=90` pooled members, or under `GATE_MIN_MODELS=3` of 4 models produces NO plays AND NO logged prediction, and is named on the header strip. Refinements vs the 0.8 draft: (1) AMENDED in batch 8 at the owner's direction: gated ladders are QUARANTINED, not dropped: the full record logs with a `gated` reason flag, carries no plays, is skipped by calibration learning, drift alarms, and every report aggregate, never overwrites a record holding frozen plays, and resolves normally so the exclusion can be judged against the settlement later (never destroy the evidence needed to audit an exclusion rule). (2) the probability-sum test was replaced by a structural check computed over ALL parsed strikes before the quote filter, which is strictly stronger for its purpose (parser or Kalshi structure breakage) and immune to the false positive of an unquoted tail bucket. Live check at ship time: 0 false positives across all 80 real ladders. Do not remove.
- **Exposure caps (audit batch 8).** After sizing, plays fill best-first (units, then p_win, then net, then ticker for determinism) into `DAILY_UNIT_CAP=6.0` per TARGET date and `EVENT_UNIT_CAP=2.0` per ladder; overflow plays are never logged (their counterfactual stays reconstructible from the stored buckets, and the header shows a "cap trimmed N" chip). Evidence: uncapped history staked 54.5u on one target date against a $500 bankroll with up to 5 plays riding one settlement number; bootstrap median max drawdown was -$255 (p95 -$461). Cross-city error concordance measured at 51% (independent), so the within-event stack is the real correlation risk. Cap pruning may only touch plays frozen during the CURRENT invocation (tracked by an explicit set, not a timestamp: two runs in one minute defeat a timestamp, unit-test proven). SEEDED as of v12: the budgets are pre-charged with every already-frozen play for each target (earlier runs today, inherited legacy records), so the caps bound CUMULATIVE frozen exposure, not per-run exposure; deploy day proved a race can inherit a 37.5u uncapped legacy board, and an unseeded second run added 3.5u on top of 4.5u inherited for Jul-7. Do not remove.
- **Tail humility clamp (audit batch 4).** Edge and p_win are computed from mp clamped into `[TAIL_FLOOR, 1-TAIL_FLOOR]` (0.015); displayed and logged mp stay RAW so the calibration tables keep seeing the true model. Evidence: buckets the model stated at 0-2% realized ~6.8% (n=118 buckets, Wilson lower bound 3.5%, misses cluster within busted ladders so treat magnitude as directional). Gaussian kernel tails are too thin for temperature busts and a fake NO edge on an ultra-thin tail passes the cost gate quietly. The clamp only ever SHRINKS a stated edge (by at most ~1.5 points). Honest counterweight: actual tail plays are 7W-2L for +$29 so far (n=9), so this is forward insurance against a measured bucket-level mispricing, not a reaction to losses. Do not remove; escalation and relaxation rules live in FUTURE.md section 2b.

### 4b. The calibration engine (DELIBERATE, self-learning)

`calib_params(state)` learns, per (city, kind), from settled results:
- **Bias correction (`corr`)**: negative of the rolling mean RAW forecast bias (most recent `BIAS_LOOKBACK` settlements BY TARGET DATE), shrunk by `n/(n+BIAS_SHRINK_K)`, zero until `BIAS_MIN_N` settlements exist. RAW bias is reconstructed as logged `bias` MINUS the `bias_corr` that was applied at log time (corrected_mean = raw_mean + corr, so raw = bias - corr), keeping the learning target stable as corrections evolve. The pre-audit code ADDED the correction instead: a stable but wrong loop whose fixed point delivers only s/(1+2s) of the needed shift (about a third at full shrink), leaving most of a station offset in place forever; fixed and simulation-verified in audit batch 3, and it never fired live because no city had reached `BIAS_MIN_N`. Do not flip the sign back. This is the honest fix for station-coordinate offsets: it comes from Kalshi's own settlements, so it works WITH the bias guard (a corrected city naturally stops tripping `BIAS_TOL`), it never manufactures edge.
- **Dressing sigma (`sigma`)**: Gaussian kernel width per Wang-Bishop second-moment matching: kernel variance = variance of realized bias-corrected errors minus mean raw member variance, clamped to [`DRESS_SIGMA_MIN`, `DRESS_SIGMA_MAX`]. Falls back to a pooled global sigma (needs 15+ settlements), then `DRESS_SIGMA_DEFAULT`. Raw member counting into 1-degree buckets is sampling noise past the first decimal; the kernel is what makes bucket probabilities smooth and honest.
- `dressed_prob` computes bucket probability as average kernel mass in [floor-0.5, cap+0.5), matching NWS half-up rounding. Ladder probabilities still sum to ~1.
- Every prediction logs `bias_corr`, `sigma`, member `sd`, predictive `psd`, and `model_version`; `resolve_pending` copies `sd`/`bias_corr`/`sigma` into resolved records so the loop closes. Old records without these fields are read defensively.
- The Results tab shows a Calibration table (forecast prob vs realized frequency by decile) and a Learned corrections table (shift, width, n per city). Those two tables are the tuning instruments; do not remove them.

## 5. Unit sizing (DELIBERATE, four guardrails)

`size_play(net, p_win, proven)` returns (units, reason). Sizing is driven by EDGE MAGNITUDE, then capped. Do not collapse these.

1. **Edge bands** set the base from net edge (model prob minus price, after spread and fee): `>= EDGE_2U (0.14)` gives 2u, `>= EDGE_1_5U (0.08)` gives 1.5u, `>= PLAY_NET_EDGE (0.04)` gives 1u, below gives no bet.
2. **Plausibility cap (`SUSPECT_EDGE`, 0.20).** A net edge above 20 points is almost always the model being wrong or a thin/stale market, NOT free money. It is capped to 1u and flagged. This is the single most important lesson the owner learned (from a real "52% edge" that was noise): a bigger edge is not a green light past a point, it is a red flag. Do NOT invert this to size UP on huge edges.
3. **Win-probability cap (`WINPROB_CAP`).** `p_win` is the model prob the POSITION wins (`mp` for YES, `1 - mp` for NO). Win < 42% of the time trims to 1.5u, < 30% to 1u; 2u needs p_win >= 0.55. A long price is already its own reward. This is why fading an overpriced YES longshot via NO (high p_win) can size up while buying the YES longshot (low p_win) cannot.
4. **Lead cap (`LEAD_CAP_DAYS`).** Plays 3+ days out are capped at 1u. Forecast skill decays fast with lead; this restores the lead discipline the old tier system had.
5. **Proven-city gate.** 2u also requires the city to appear in `city_skill` (>= 20 resolved buckets with a positive Brier edge). Until then 2u is locked to 1.5u. Early on nothing is proven, so 1.5u is effectively the ceiling. Intended.

`SUSPECT_EDGE`, `EDGE_2U`, `EDGE_1_5U`, `WINPROB_CAP`, `PLAY_NET_EDGE`, `LEAD_CAP_DAYS` are tunable constants at the top of the file; retune them from the by-edge and by-unit tables on the Results tab as data accumulates. `tier_for`/`units_of` remain defined but no longer drive sizing (the S/A/B tag shown is derived from the final unit size). `city_skill` still uses realized Brier, not tier.

## 6. Data sources and quirks

- **Kalshi** (`api.elections.kalshi.com/trade-api/v2`): no auth needed for reads. `events?...&with_nested_markets=true` for open markets; `markets?event_ticker=...&status=settled` for settlement. Settled markets expose `result` ("yes"/"no") and `expiration_value` (the actual settled temperature). Buckets carry `floor_strike`, `cap_strike`, `strike_type` ("less"/"between"/"greater"); parse those, not the title text.
- **Fees (audit batch 6, authoritative).** This series' fee is `fee_type: "quadratic"`, `fee_multiplier: 1`, read live from `GET /series/{ticker}`: taker fee = 0.07 x contracts x price x (1-price), rounded UP to the next cent per TRADE. `fee()` returns the unrounded rate for the cost gate; `resolve_pending` applies the trade-level ceil. Maker fees: none observed for this series; settlement fee: none. kalshi.com itself rate-limited our runner, so the round-up convention follows Kalshi's published schedule; ceil can only overstate paper costs, the conservative direction.
- **Two ticker generations (audit batch 1).** Newer cities use `KXHIGHT*`/`KXLOWT*`; the seven original cities' HIGH series are legacy `KXHIGH*` without the T (`KXHIGHAUS/CHI/DEN/LAX/MIA/PHIL` and `KXHIGHNY`, aliased NY -> NYC via `SERIES_ALIAS`). Matching only `KXHIGHT*` silently dropped 7 of 20 HIGH ladders, including the most liquid, from v1 through v3. All LOW series are on `KXLOWT*`.
- **Next-day listing time.** Kalshi opens a target day's markets at 14:00 UTC the day BEFORE (`open_time`), and markets stay open until ~06:00 UTC after the target day. A run before 14:00 UTC can therefore never see tomorrow; that is why the 14:43 UTC capture cron exists. Same-day LOWs are realized-guarded, so LOW markets are ONLY ever logged by a run that lands between 14:00 UTC the prior day and the city's local midnight.
- **Settlement stations verified 2026-07-04** against each market's `rules_secondary` CLI product: all 20 match `CITIES` coordinates except Houston, which settles at HOBBY (`CLIHOU`), not IAH; fixed in audit batch 1. NYC is `CLINYC` Central Park, CHI is `CLIMDW` Midway, AUS is `CLIAUS` Bergstrom, PHIL is `CLIPHL`. Re-verify if Kalshi changes rules text.
- **GitHub Pages can serve a stale board.** Observed 2026-07-04: Pages served the previous run's board (showing +$56.47) while the repo's committed board was current (-$52.99), because the Pages deploy for the newest commit had not landed. The pages now carry their build epoch and self-display a staleness banner past 16h; treat a visible banner as "do not bet from this board."
- **Open-Meteo ensemble** (`ensemble-api.open-meteo.com`): free, no key, `models=gfs025,ecmwf_ifs025,icon_seamless,gem_global` pooled for ~143 members. Returns hourly per-member temps localized to the requested `timezone` in local CLOCK time. Do NOT group by `t[:10]` directly: `fetch_members` shifts timestamps back to Local Standard Time (using `utc_offset_seconds` minus the city's fixed standard offset in `STD_OFFSET_H`) before picking daily highs/lows, because NWS Climate Reports (Kalshi's settlement source) use LST year-round. Removing that shift silently breaks LOW markets during DST. `utc_offset_seconds` is also used for the timing guard.
- **Reference forecasts + run provenance (audit batch 2).** Every run also logs NBM and HRRR point forecasts (`REF_MODELS`, forecast API `api.open-meteo.com`, same LST windowing, stored in each record's `ref`) and the init time of the newest run per model (`model_runs`, from `api.open-meteo.com/data/{META_IDS[model]}/static/meta.json`; forecast responses carry no run id). These are evidence-only: they must never touch scoring, sizing, or guards until a batch 4/5 decision says so. Measured availability lags (2026-07-05): GEFS init+5.7h, ECMWF ENS init+8.2h (the slowest), ICON-EPS init+3.7h (00/12z only), GEM ENS init+6.6h (00/12z only), NBM ~+1.1h hourly, HRRR ~+1.6h hourly. Cron consequence: the 12:17 UTC run rides the 06z/00z cycle, 21:38 UTC has the complete 12z cycle (in by ~20:12), 02:07 UTC adds 18z GFS. Usage is roughly 320 calls/day against Open-Meteo's 10,000/day free tier.
- **City -> station coordinates** in `CITIES` are approximate (airport or Central Park). A wrong coordinate shows up as a persistent per-city bias on the Results tab and is auto-suppressed by the bias guard. Fixing a city means correcting its lat/lon to Kalshi's actual resolution station.
- **`CI` flag:** when `os.environ["CI"] == "true"` (GitHub Actions sets this), the script does not open a browser or wait for input. Keep it cloud-runnable and non-interactive.
- **Python version:** the workflow pins 3.12. Backslashes inside f-string expressions are a SyntaxError on 3.11 and earlier; avoid them (use a named constant like `DOT`) so the file stays portable. This exact bug broke a run once.

## 7. Outputs and persistence

Each run writes:
- `docs/index.html` (today's bets) and `docs/results.html` (tracker). GitHub Pages serves `/docs`.
- `weather_state.json`: `{"predictions": {...}, "resolved": [...]}`. Predictions are logged only for non-realized markets (genuine forecasts). Resolved records hold per-bucket hit, per-play win/pnl/margin, and forecast bias.
- Since audit batch 1, predictions also carry `first_logged`, explicit-UTC `logged_at` (`...Z`), and, once plays exist, frozen `plays_lead` / `plays_logged_at` / `plays_model_version`. Resolved records carry record-level `model_version` and `first_logged`, and each resolved play carries its decision-time `lead`. All of these are read defensively; old records simply lack them.

On GitHub the workflow commits these back so state persists across ephemeral runs and time off. Do not move persistence to anything needing a database or paid service; the commit-back pattern is deliberate and free.

**NEVER delete or overwrite `weather_state.json`**; it is the entire track record, and the zip handed to the owner deliberately excludes it. Every logged bet is stamped with `MODEL_VERSION` and stores its own edge, so retunes start a new version alongside old results rather than invalidating them. New report fields must read old records defensively (`.get`, guard `None`) so an upgrade never breaks or loses history. Git commit history of the state file is the backup of record.

### 7b. State schema reference + archive policy (audit batch 3)

`predictions[key]`, key = `CODE|KIND|YYYY-MM-DD`. Required fields: code, kind, target, event_ticker, logged_at, lead, mean, sd, psd, bias_corr, sigma, model_version, biased, offset, buckets[], plays[]. Era-optional (always `.get`): `first_logged` (v4+); `plays_lead` / `plays_logged_at` / `plays_model_version` (v4+, present once plays freeze); `model_runs` / `members_by_model` / `ref` (v5+).
- `buckets[i]`: ticker, bid, sub, floor, cap, stype, mp, mid, yb, ya, oi (market prices as of the record's LAST refresh).
- `plays[i]`: ticker, bid, sub, side, entry, net, edge, tier, units, stake, p_win, mp, mid (frozen at first non-empty log). Resolved plays add v7+: `close_mid` (mid at the final actionable board, the last refresh of the record) and `clv` (signed movement toward the position between the frozen entry board and that final board).
- `resolved[i]`: code, kind, target, lead, actual, mean, bias, sd, bias_corr, sigma, buckets[{mp, mid, hit}], plays[+won, pnl, margin, contracts, actual, lead, model_version]; v4+: record-level model_version, first_logged; v5+: model_runs, members_by_model, ref; v6+: psd, crps (closed-form Gaussian CRPS from mean/psd), and bucket-level rep (ordering midpoint, enables RPS-style ordered scores retroactively).

Contract: every reader of old records uses `.get` with fallbacks; adding a REQUIRED field without a migration note here is forbidden. v13+ adds an optional `nowcast` dict on same-day HIGH predictions (write-once paired shadow snapshot: asof, obs_max, n_obs, mean_u/psd_u, mean_t/psd_t, buckets[{ticker, rep, mp_u, mp_t}], model_version, cfg) and, once graded at resolve, a compact `nowcast` dict on the resolved record (asof, obs_max, n_obs, mean_u, mean_t, rps_u, rps_t, crps_u, crps_t); per-bucket shadow detail dies with the pending record by design. v11+ adds `cfg` on predictions and resolved records (8-hex CONFIG_HASH of the knob block: a knob edited without a version bump still splits cleanly in analysis). v10+ adds `mean_hist` on predictions and resolved records: up to the last 6 [run_stamp, mean] pairs, the run-by-run forecast revision trail (feeds the surprise detector and run-timing analyses). v9+ adds an optional `gated` reason string on quarantined prediction and resolved records. v8+ adds one optional top-level state key: `calib_snapshot` (map of CODE|KIND to the last run's applied correction, used only by the drift alarms).
